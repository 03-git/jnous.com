FIX A RUNNING TOILET

Steps:
1. Remove the tank lid. Set it on a towel — porcelain chips easily.
2. Check the flapper: push down on it with your finger. If water stops, the flapper is the problem.
3. Replace flapper: unhook the old one from the overflow tube ears. Take it to the store for size match. Hook the new one on, reconnect the chain with slight slack.
4. If flapper is fine, check the fill valve. Flush and watch — if water rises above the overflow tube, the float is set too high.
5. Adjust float: turn the screw on top of the fill valve counterclockwise to lower the water level. Water should stop 1 inch below the overflow tube top.
6. If fill valve won't shut off at any float level, replace it. Turn off the supply valve. Flush to empty tank. Unscrew the locknut under the tank. Swap the valve. Hand-tighten the locknut.
7. Turn water back on. Watch one full fill cycle to confirm it stops.

Notes:
- Flappers degrade over time. This is the most common cause.
- Universal flappers fit most toilets. Bring the old one to match size.
- If the porcelain tank is cracked, do not repair it. Replace the toilet.
- Call a plumber if: water is leaking from the base (wax ring failure, not a tank issue).
