SAFE PUBLIC WIFI USAGE

Steps:
1. Assume all public wifi is hostile. Anyone on the same network can potentially see your traffic.
2. USE A VPN: A VPN encrypts all your traffic so the network operator and other users cannot see it. Use a reputable paid VPN (Mullvad, ProtonVPN, IVPN). Avoid free VPNs — they often sell your data.
3. VERIFY THE NETWORK NAME: Ask staff for the exact network name. Attackers create fake networks with similar names ("Starbucks_Free_WiFi" vs "Starbucks WiFi").
4. DISABLE AUTO-CONNECT: Turn off automatic wifi connections on your phone and laptop. Connect manually and only when needed.
5. USE HTTPS ONLY: Look for the lock icon in your browser. Avoid entering passwords or financial info on non-HTTPS sites. Enable HTTPS-only mode in your browser settings (built into Firefox, Brave, and Chrome).
6. DO NOT: access banking, enter credit card numbers, or log into sensitive accounts on public wifi without a VPN.
7. FORGET THE NETWORK when you're done. On both phone and laptop, remove the network from saved connections.
8. Turn off file sharing and AirDrop while on public networks.

Notes:
- Your phone's cellular data is significantly safer than public wifi. Use your phone as a hotspot if you need to do sensitive work on a laptop.
- If you don't have a VPN and must use public wifi: limit activity to non-sensitive browsing only.
