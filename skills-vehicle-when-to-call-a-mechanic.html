WHEN TO CALL A MECHANIC (DECISION TREE)
Steps:
1. Is anyone at risk? Brake failure, steering loss, smoke/fire, fuel leak → STOP DRIVING. Tow to shop.
2. Is the check engine light flashing? → Misfire damaging catalytic converter. Do not drive. Tow.
3. Is it a safety system? Brakes, steering, suspension, airbags → mechanic. Mistakes here kill.
4. Does it require specialized tools? Timing belt, transmission, AC refrigerant → mechanic.
5. Does it require calibration? ADAS cameras, torque-to-yield bolts, alignment → mechanic.
6. Are you unsure of the diagnosis? → Read OBD codes first. If still unclear, mechanic.
7. Is the part accessible and low-risk? Filters, wipers, bulbs, battery, fluids → DIY is fine.
8. Do you have the space, tools, and time? No → mechanic. False economy to half-finish a job.
Notes:
- "I watched a YouTube video" is not a substitute for experience on safety-critical systems.
- If a DIY job goes wrong mid-way, stop and tow. Don't force it.
- Second opinions are cheap. Engine/transmission rebuilds are not.
