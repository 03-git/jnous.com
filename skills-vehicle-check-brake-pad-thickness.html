CHECK BRAKE PAD THICKNESS
Steps:
1. Vehicle on level ground. Wheels turned for access (or remove wheel).
2. Look through spokes at the brake caliper.
3. Find the pad pressed against the rotor. Measure visible friction material.
4. Minimum safe: 3mm (~1/8 inch). New pads are typically 10-12mm.
5. Check both sides of each axle. Uneven wear means caliper issue.
6. Listen for wear indicators: high-pitched squeal = pads at limit.
7. If grinding (metal on metal): stop driving. Rotor damage is occurring.
Notes:
- Pads below 3mm: schedule replacement within days, not weeks.
- If you're not sure what you're looking at, take a photo and compare to a reference image.
- Rear pads typically last longer than fronts. Check all four.
