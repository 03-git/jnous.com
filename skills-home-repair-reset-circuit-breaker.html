RESET A TRIPPED CIRCUIT BREAKER

Steps:
1. Go to your electrical panel. It's usually in the garage, basement, utility room, or exterior wall.
2. Open the panel door. Look for a breaker that is in the middle position (not fully ON or OFF), or has an orange/red indicator showing.
3. Push the tripped breaker firmly to the full OFF position first. You should feel it click.
4. Then push it firmly to the ON position. It should click and hold.
5. Check if power is restored to the affected area.
6. If the breaker trips again immediately, do not reset it again. See "diagnose why a circuit keeps tripping."

Notes:
- Stand to the side of the panel when resetting, not directly in front.
- Dry your hands first. Stand on a dry surface.
- A breaker that won't stay in the ON position has an active fault. Do not force it.
- If the main breaker (large, top or bottom) has tripped, reset it the same way. If it trips again, call an electrician.
- GFCI and AFCI breakers have a test button on the breaker itself. After resetting, press TEST then RESET to verify protection is working.
