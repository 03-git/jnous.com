WHEN TO CALL A PLUMBER (DECISION TREE)

Call immediately — do not attempt:
- Water is spraying or flooding and you cannot find or close the shutoff valve.
- Sewage is backing up into the house (any fixture).
- You smell gas near a water heater. Leave the house. Call the gas company first, then a plumber.
- A pipe has burst inside a wall or ceiling.
- Hot water heater is leaking from the tank body (not a fitting).

Call soon — not a DIY job:
- Main sewer line is clogged (multiple fixtures back up simultaneously).
- Water heater needs replacement.
- Frozen pipes that have not yet burst (a plumber can thaw safely).
- Any work requiring a permit: moving/adding supply lines, moving drains, installing a water heater.
- Slab leak (water pooling on floor with no visible source).
- Low water pressure throughout the whole house after checking the main valve.
- Polybutylene (gray plastic) pipe — requires full replacement.

Fix it yourself first:
- Single clogged sink or toilet → see: unclog-drain
- Running toilet → see: fix-running-toilet
- Dripping faucet → see: fix-leaky-faucet
- Stuck shutoff valve (if upstream shutoff is accessible) → see: water-shutoff-valve

Notes:
- When in doubt, shut off the water and call. Water damage costs more than a service call.
- Get quotes from 2-3 plumbers for non-emergency work.
- Licensed plumber required for permit work in most jurisdictions.
