REMOVE PERSONAL INFO FROM DATA BROKERS

Steps:
1. Search for yourself: Google your full name, phone number, and address. Note which sites show your data (Spokeo, WhitePages, BeenVerified, Radaris, etc.).
2. For each site, search for their opt-out page. Common data broker sites: Spokeo, WhitePages, BeenVerified, Radaris, PeopleFinder. Search "[site name] opt out" to find current removal pages.
3. Each site has a different process — typically: find your listing, request removal, confirm via email.
4. Repeat for every site that has your info. There are dozens of data broker sites.
5. Re-check in 2-4 weeks. Some sites re-add data. You may need to repeat.
6. To automate: paid services like DeleteMe or Optery handle ongoing removal for you.

Notes:
- This is a recurring task, not a one-time fix. Data brokers buy and re-list information continuously.
- Some sites require you to create an account or submit ID to opt out — this is legitimate but frustrating.
- Going forward: minimize sharing your real phone number and home address. Use a PO Box or virtual mailbox and a secondary phone number (Google Voice) for online forms.
